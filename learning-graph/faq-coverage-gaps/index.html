Summary

The current FAQ covers 114 of 205 concepts from the learning graph, achieving 55.6% coverage. This report identifies the 91 uncovered concepts, classifies them by priority, and recommends which gaps to address first. Critical gaps represent foundational concepts that underpin many others in the curriculum — leaving these unanswered creates structural holes in the reader's understanding. Medium priority gaps address important but more targeted topics, while low priority gaps are specialized or leaf-level concepts that serve narrower audiences. Addressing the top critical and medium gaps would raise FAQ coverage to approximately 80% and significantly improve the resource's usefulness as a standalone reference.

Recommendations

Priority 1 — Address Critical Gaps Immediately

The 22 critical gaps represent foundational knowledge that readers need before they can benefit from many other FAQ answers. Recommended order of resolution:

  1. Money Mindset and Money Scripts (Ch. 1) — These set the psychological frame for the entire book and are referenced throughout.
  2. Financial Goals and SMART Goals (Ch. 1) — Almost every action item in the book assumes the reader can set and track goals.
  3. Needs vs Wants and Expense Tracking (Ch. 2) — Core mechanics of the budgeting chapter cannot be applied without these concepts.
  4. Risk vs Return, Time Value of Money, Asset Allocation (Ch. 8) — These three form the conceptual backbone of all investing content.
  5. Marginal Tax Rate, Tax Deductions, Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Ch. 7) — Prerequisite for understanding Roth IRA, 401(k), and HSA content.
  6. Simple Interest and Minimum Payment Trap (Ch. 6) — Essential for readers dealing with credit card or loan debt.
  7. Three Month / Six Month Expenses and Financial Safety Net (Ch. 5) — Emergency fund sizing is one of the most common reader questions.
  8. Loss Aversion and Anchoring Bias (Ch. 18) — Behavioral concepts that explain why readers fail to execute good plans.

Priority 2 — Close Medium Gaps in High-Traffic Chapters

Focus next on chapters with the most medium-priority gaps:

  • Chapter 7 (Taxes): Five medium gaps (W-2, W-4, 1099, State Income Tax, Tax Refund) — these are highly searchable, seasonal topics.
  • Chapter 9 (Stock Market): Five medium gaps (Dow Jones, Market Cap, Bull/Bear, Stock Exchange, Buy and Hold) — frequently asked by new investors.
  • Chapter 10 (Roth IRA): Five medium gaps (Contribution Limits, Tax-Deferred Growth, RMDs, Target Date Fund, Three-Fund Portfolio).
  • Chapter 18 (Behavioral Finance & Scams): Five medium gaps (Confirmation Bias, Financial Scams, Phishing, Identity Theft, Social Media Misinfo) — high relevance for young adults.

Priority 3 — Address Low Priority Gaps as Supplementary Content

The five cryptocurrency-related low-priority gaps (Blockchain, Crypto Volatility, Portfolio Allocation, NFTs, Crypto Exchanges) can be consolidated into a single extended FAQ answer or a dedicated "Crypto FAQ" section rather than individual entries.

Structural Recommendation

Consider organizing FAQ answers by chapter to match the book's structure. Readers progressing through the book should be able to find answers to chapter-specific questions without searching the entire FAQ. A chapter-indexed FAQ would also make coverage gaps visible at a glance during maintenance.
